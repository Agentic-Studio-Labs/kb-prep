Teachers should create an anchor chart together as a class on chart paper to reinforce the concepts used in the financial literacy lesson "How Can SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound) Goals Help You Achieve Your Dreams?" for grades 4-5. This anchor chart is a visual teaching tool that should be displayed throughout the lesson activities and unit. When students have questions about SMART goal concepts (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound criteria), teachers can refer them back to the anchor chart and use it as a teaching tool.

In each of the boxes write a question students can ask themself to ensure their goal meets that element. Students should brainstorm and create these questions themselves. However, examples are included in each box. The anchor chart created in class can also include drawings, term definitions, and examples of the components of a SMART Goal. Allow students to volunteer the definitions and examples of each term based on what they have learned and include them in the chart.
